Base Organ Blood Flow

The base organs are all organs except skeletal muscle and adipose tissue. The assumption is that blood flow in the base organs is only size specific and is not dependent on gender, muscularity and adiposity.

Using this assumption we only need to know base organ mass and blood flow per gram to scale the base organs blood flow. For cardiac output work, we include the GI tract organ weight in the sums but not the blood flow -- since blood flow through the liver is counted.

The values for our 75 kG male are blood flow is 3931 mL/Min, organ mass is 49,515 G and flow per gram is 0.079.
