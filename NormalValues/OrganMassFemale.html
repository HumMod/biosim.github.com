Normal Values > Organ Mass - Female

Basic Considerations

We can use the base organ mass concept here to calculate organ mass in the female. Compared to the male, the female will have a smaller body mass, a smaller fraction of body mass in skeletal muscle and a greater fraction of body mass in adipose tissue.

Body Mass

Detailed elsewhere, typical female body mass is 60.5 kG (133 Lbs).

Skeletal Muscle

My data here is skimpy. Physiological Parameter Values ... 1994 reports the skeletal muscle is 40% of body weight in males and 29% in females. This yields a total muscle mass of 17.5 kG in our 60.5 kG female.

I used 29% for total muscle and estimated respiratory muscle at 2900G. This produced skeletal muscle values of 24.2% and 14,630 G.

Adipose Tissue

Clarys etal report adipose tissue mass of 13.6 +/- 5.3 % of body weight in 42.4 year old males.

Reference Man suggests 21.3% of body weight in males and 32.7% in females.

I used 32.7% for adipose tissue mass and got a value of 19,767 G.

Body Fluids

Body fluids include blood, fluid in the peritoneal cavity, excess lung water and fluid in the gut.

Using 8.7% of body weight (same as the male), body fluids are 5260 G.

Base Organ Mass

We can now calculated the base organ mass as body weight minus the sum of skeletal muscle mass, fat mass and body fluid mass. The result value is 20,795 G. This mass can now be partitioned among the ten organs that share base organ mass.
