<topicdoc>
<name> Conductance - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Conductance is a measure of the blood flow through
a vessel for a given pressure difference. It is the
exact reciprocal of resistance. Since the arteries have
more resistance than the veins, conductance is less in
the arterial side of the circulation.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> BVSeqArtys - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Sequestered blood volume is a term used to explain the
changes in blood volume distribution and venous return
when a subject moves from a lying to a standing postion.
As the subject changes from a lying to standing position,
total blood volume does not acutely change, however
venous return falls as if some blood has been lost. The
(virtual) amount of blood that appears to be lost is called
sequestered. Some is in the arteries (BVSeqArtys) and some
in the veins (BVSeqVeins) of the lower extremities.
In the model we assume about 75% of sequestered volume is
in the veins and and 25% in the arteries.

In the lying position, assume a pressure of 97mmHg in the
arteries and a sequestered volume of 50mL. Moving to a
standing position changes pressure to 150mmHg and the
sequestered volume to 200mL.

Vascular compliance plays a role in sequestration since
the amount sequestered depends on the distending (orthostatic)
pressure versus the vascular compliance pushing back.
Together they affect the transmural pressure(TMP)

<topicdoc>
<name> TMP - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>
The transmural pressure is the difference between the pressure
within the vessel and outside the vessel. It is a function of
vascular compliance, the orthostatic pressure and any other
pressure exerted externally(lower external pressure).
