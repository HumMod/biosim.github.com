<topicdoc>
<name> Leptin - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Leptin is a 167 - amino acid peptide protein synthesized
predominantly in adipose tissue. It is the product of the
ob gene and its name is derived from the greek word
"leptos" which means thin. Mutation or deletion of the ob
gene leads to obesity. Leptin acts via hypothalamic receptors
to inhibit feeding and increase thermogeneis.

Reviews
Gautron L, Elmquist JK. Sixteen years and counting:
an update on leptin in energy balance.
J Clin Invest 121: 2087 - 2093, 2011.

Mantzoros CS, Magkos F, Brinkoetter M, Sienkiewicz E,
Dardeno TA, Kim SY, Hamnvik OP, Koniaris A.
Leptin in human physiology and pathophysiology.
Am J Physiol Endocrinol Metab 301: E567 - E584, 2011.

Jequier E. Leptin signaling, adiposity, and energy balance.
Ann N.Y. Acad Sci 967: 379 - 388, 2002.

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - DefinitionLP2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in the leptin pool or mass are a function of both
gains and losses in leptin over time. Gains may occur from
increased secretion and from infusion of leptin using the
leptin pump feature of the model. Loss occurs from leptin
clearance from the blood


</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - DefinitionLP3 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Gain is defined as positive changes in mass of leptin
and is a function of leptin secretion rate and leptin
from infusion (using the leptin pump feature).

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialMass - DefinitionLP4 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

During startup, the model user defines the particular
traits of the HumMod patient. These are gender, body size,
age, muscularity and adiposity. All of these traits
influence body volumes, most notably the extracellular
fluid volume (ECFV). The assumption is made that
while volumes may change with traits, concentrations do
not. At startup,initial volumes,especially ECFV and plasma
volume are calculated. A target concentration is specified
- in this case Target[Leptin(nG/mL)].

The initial mass or circulating pool is the Target[Leptin]
multiplied by the initial volume of distribution, in
this case the ECFV.

Initial Mass = Target[Leptin(nG/mL)]*ECFV.InitialVol(L)

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - DefinitionLP5 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Loss is defined as negative changes in mass of leptin and is
a function of leptin clearance rate.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Mass - DefinitionLP6 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The Mass is the calculated amount of Leptin at any given time.
It is a function of the initial mass of Leptin and the change
in Leptin mass over time.


</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Leptin - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

Plasma leptin levels correlate highly with percentage body
fat, increasing exponentially with increasing body mass
index (BMI). There is a great deal of interindividual
variability in plasma leptin concentration and gender is also
an important factor, with women having markedly higher leptin
concentrations than men for any given degree of fat mass.

In HumMod, plasma leptin concentartion is 8.0 nG/mL

Human Data
Reference (1)
Mean (+/- SD) plasma concentrations of leptin were
7.5 +/- 9.3 nG/mL (n = 27) in normal weight subjects and
31.3 +/- 24.1 nG/mL (n = 27) in obese subjects. In this
particular study, men and women were grouped together.

Considine RV, Sinha MK, Heiman ML, Kriauciunas A,
Stephens TW, Nyce MR, Ohannesian JP, Marco CC, McKee LJ,
Bauer TL, Caro JF. Serum immunoreactive-leptin
concentrations in normal-weight and obese humans.
N Engl J Med 334; 292 - 295, 1996.

Reference (2)
Men (n = 19) Leptin levels ranged between 1.8 - 14.4 nG/mL
Women (n = 11) Leptin levels ranged between 7.2 - 28.9 nG/mL
Fat Mass = 14 -16 Kg

Khan A, Boyadijan R, Jinagouda SD, el-Tawil K, Rude RK,
Kamdr V. Sexual dimorphism in plasma leptin concentration.
J Cil Endocrinol Metab 82: 579 - 584, 1997.
