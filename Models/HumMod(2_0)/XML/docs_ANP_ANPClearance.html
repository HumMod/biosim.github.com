<topicdoc>
<name> ANPClearance - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

ANP is eliminated from the circulation by almost every tissue
by a combination of receptor mediated binding, by uptake and
metabolism by target tissues and by degradation by enzymes,
notably endopeptidases, and excretion into non plasma fluids
such as urine. The majority of ANP clearance probably involves
a receptor-mediated endocytosis. Two main types of ANP-specific
receptors have been identified - guanylate-cyclase-linked receptors
and a receptor thought to have a clearance function. The latter
(the clearance ANP receptors) comprise the largest population of
receptors and they appear to serve as a powerful mechanism
to clear ANP from the circulaton.

The relative contributions of different organs to the extraction
of ANP clearance have been studied systematically in both
experimental animals and humans. The lungs, kidneys and liver
are all important for ANP clearance.
