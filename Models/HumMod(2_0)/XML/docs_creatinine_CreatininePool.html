<topicdoc>
<name> Creatinine - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Creatinine is formed by nonenzymatic dehydration of
creatine and creatine phosphate within striated,
smooth and cardiac muscle.
Creatine in the body arises from two sources:
endogenous synthesis from amino acids and creatine
in the diet, notably from the ingestion of meat.
Endogenous synthesis occurs primarliy in the kidney
but also occurs in the liver. Creatine is concentrated
and stored mainly in skeletal muscle. Approximately
90% of the body stores of creatine are contained in
skeletal muscle. Creatine leaks into the extracellular
fluid where it is converted to creatinine.

Creatinine is excreted both renally and extrarenally.
Extrarenal degradation probably occurs in the intestine
by intestinal bacteria. Extrarenal excretion is about 1.5%
of total excretion and varies with plasma creatinine
concentration.

and the origin of urinary creatinine.
J. Biol. Chem. 168: 493 - 510, 1947.

Van Pilsum JF, Bovis M. Effect of various protein precipitants
on recoveries of creatinine added to plasma.
Clin Chem 3: 90 - 94, 1957.

Conn RB, Jr. Fluorimetric determination of creatine.
Clin Chem 6: 537 - 548, 1960.

Jones JD, Burnett PC. Creatinine metabolism in humans with
decreased renal function: creatinine deficit.
Clin Chem 20: 1204 - 1212, 1974.

chronic renal failure. Clin Sci (Lond) 58: 327 - 335, 1980.

excretion in young and elderly healthy subjects and in patients
with renal disease. J Am Soc Nephrol 2:856 -865, 1991.

<label> Physical Units </label>
<content>

[Plasma] mG/dL or uMol/L
mG * 0.00884 = mMol
mMol * 113.1 = mG
Mass mG
Clearance mG/min

milli = 10^-3
micro = 10^-6

<name> [Creatinine] - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

Creatinine (C4 H7 N3 O) has a molecular weight
of 113.12.

Typical plasma concentration is 0.82 mG/dL or
72.5 uMol/L

Creatinine excretion is 1.26dL/Min

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - DefinitionCP2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in the creatinine pool or mass are a function of both
gains and losses in creatinine over time. Gains may occur from
increases in the conversion of creatine to creatinine. Loss
occurs from renal creatinine clearance and nonrenal creatinine
clearance.

</content>
</topicdoc>


<topicdoc>
<name> ExtrarenalLoss - DefinitionCP3 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Extrarenal clearance has been reported to be 0.038L/Day/kG.
For a 77KG subject with a plasma concentration of 0.82mG/dL,
this yields a rate constant of 0.02 dL/Min.

ExtrarenalLoss is therefore calculated as
0.02 * [Creatinine(mG/dL)]

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialMass - DefinitionCP5 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

During startup, the model user defines the particular
traits of the HumMod patient. These are gender, body size,
age, muscularity and adiposity. All of these traits
influence body volumes, most notably total body water,
the extracellular fluid volume (ECFV) and plasma volume.
The assumption is made that while volumes may change with
traits, concentrations do not. At startup,initial volumes,
especially total body water, ECFV and plasma volume are
calculated. A target concentration is specified - in this
case Target[Creatinine].

The initial mass or circulating pool is the
Target[Creatinine] multiplied by the initial
volume of distribution.

Initial Mass = Target[Creatinine]*InitialVolDist

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialVolDist - DefinitionCP6 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

InitialVolDist is initial volume in which creatinine is
distributed. It is a fraction of total body water and is
defined by the parameter VoldDistFract.

InitialVolDist = VoldDistFract * BodyH20.InitialTotalVolume

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> VolDist - DefinitionCP13 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The volume of distribution of creatinine is 49% of body weight.
With a body weight of 77KG and a total body water of 43.2L, the
volume of distribution is 33.7L or 0.87 times total body water.

VolDist is the calculated volume of distribution.

VolDist = VolDistFract * BodyH20.TotalVol

Rowe JW, Andres R, Tobin JD, Norris AH, Shock NW. The effect
of age on creatinine clearance in men: a cross-sectional and
longitudinal study. J. Gerontology 31: 155 - 163, 1976.

Mitch We, Collier VU, Walser M. Creatinine metabolism in chronic
renal failure. Clin Sci (Lond) 58: 327 - 335, 1980.


</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> VolDistFract - DefinitionCP14 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

VolDistFract is a parameter used to calculate the volume of
distribution for creatinine. The volume of distribution of
creatinine is 49% of body weight. With a body weight of 77KG
and a total body water of 43.2L, the volume of distribution
is 33.7L or 0.87 times total body water. VolDistFract
therefore has a value of 0.87.
