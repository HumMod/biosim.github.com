<topicdoc>
<name> [K+] - KC1Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Control of potassium distribution between the extracellular
and intracellular compartments plays an important role in
potassium homeostasis. Because over 98 percent of the total body
potassium is contained in the cells, they can serve as an
overflow site for excess extracellular fluid potassium during
hyperkalemia or as a source of potassium during hypokalemia.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> [K+] - KC3Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

Extracellular K+ = 4.4 mEq/L
Intracellular K+ = 142 mEq/L

Total body K+ = 50mEq/KG. Scaling for a 75KG man,
total body K+ = 50mEq/KG * 75KG = 3750mEq

Extracellular Mass of K+ = 4.4mEq/L * 15L (ECFV) = 66mEq
Intracellular Mass of K+ = 3750 - 66 = 3684mEq

Forbes GB, Lewis AM. Total sodium, potassium and chloride
in adult man. J Clin Invest 35: 596 - 600, 1956

Pierson RN Jr., Lin DH, Phillips RA. Total-body potassium in
health: effects of age, sex, height and fat.
Am J Physiol 226: 206 - 212, 1974.

Hollifield JW, Slaton PE. Thiazide diuretics hypokalemia and
cardiac arrhythmias. Acta Med Scand Suppl 647:67 - 73, 1981

</content>
</topicdoc>


<topicdoc>
<name> CaptiveMass - KCDefinition1 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Not all of the potassium contained in cells is readily
available for flux. From depletion studies it appears
that 55% of cell K+ is captive. If we assume an initial
mass of 3964mEq, then the captive mass is 3964 * 0.55
= 2180 mEq.

Hollifield JW, Slaton PE. Thiazide diuretics hypokalemia and
cardiac arrhythmias. Acta Med Scand Suppl 647:67 - 73, 1981

Sterns RH, Cox M, Feig PU, Singer I. Internal potassium
balance and the control of the plasma potassium concentration.
Medicine 60: 339 - 354, 1981.

</content>
</topicdoc>
