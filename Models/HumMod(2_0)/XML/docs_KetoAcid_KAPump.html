<topicdoc>
<name> KAump - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The KAPump enables the model user to change
[KA] levels by infusing ketoacids at different
rates. Data is taken from a variety of human and
animal studies relating infusion rates to plasma
concentrations of KA.

When the switch is turned on, the model user sets the
rate by changing the setting using the slide bar.

Dog Data
Madison LL, Mebane D, Unger RH, Lochner A. The hypoglycemic
action of ketones. II Evidence for a stimulatory feedback
of ketones on the pancreatic beta cells.
J Clin Invest 43: 408 - 415, 1964.

Bjorntorp P, Schersten T. Effect of beta-hydroxybutyrate
on lipid mobilization. Am J Physiol 212: 683 - 687, 1967.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Setting - DefinitionKA2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

When the KAPump switch is turned on, the model
user can set infusion rates using the sliding bar
feature in the KA pump display box. Rates of infusion
may be increased from 0 in increments of 1, up to
10mG/Min; from 10 mG/Min, in increments of 10, up to
100 mG/Min; and from 100 mG/Min in increments of 100
up to 1000 mG/Min.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> KA - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

The basic mass units for ketoacids are mMol. We
can then use the mass in osmoles calculations with
no modification.

We assume an average molecular weight of 102.

1 mMol/L = 10.2 mG/dL

Normal [KA-] is 0.05 mMol/L.

The volume of distribution is ECFV or 15.0 L. Thus,
the nominal KA mass is 0.75 mMol.

</content>
</topicdoc>
