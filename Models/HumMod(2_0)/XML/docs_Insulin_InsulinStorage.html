<topicdoc>
<name> InsulinStorage - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Insulin is synthesized in the islet beta cells of the
pancreas and stored in secretory granules within the
cytoplasm. Recent studies utilizing both capacitance
measurements and electron microscopy of pancreatic beta
cells suggests that the secretory granules are divided
into two distinct functional pools - a readily releasable
pool of granules that is docked at the plasma membrane
and can undergo exocytosis immediately when the cell is
stimulated, and a reserve pool located in the cytoplasm
which is recruited to replenish the readily releasable
pool. This correlates well with the biphasic release of
insulin seen in healthy individuals. The first phase reflects
exocytosis of the readily releasable pool of granules while
the second phase is due to release of the reserve pool of
granules. Most of the granules (95%) belong to reserve pool
and need to be chemically modified to become immediately
available for release. The mobilization of the reserve
pool granules into the readily releasable pool involves
one or several ATP -dependent reactions.

Rorsman P, Eliasson L, Renstrom E, Gromada J, Barg S,
Gopel S. The cell physiology of biphasic insulin secretion.
News Physiol Sci 15: 72 - 77, 2000

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Mass - Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The insulin mass is the calculated amount of insulin present
in the beta cells of the pancreas.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The gain in insulin storage is defined as the positive
change in insulin mass in the pancreas as a result of
insulin synthesis.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The loss in insulin storage is defined as the negative
change in insulin mass in the pancreas as insulin is
secreted.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The change in insulin storage is defined as the change in
mass of insulin in the pancreas as a result of secretion
(loss) and synthesis (gain).

</content>
</topicdoc>
