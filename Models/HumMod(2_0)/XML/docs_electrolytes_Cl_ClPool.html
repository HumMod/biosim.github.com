<topicdoc>
<name> [Cl-] - ClPOverview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The extracellular fluid contains large amounts of chloride ions
and plasma chloride is normally regulated within very narrow limits.

Textbook of Medical Physiology AC Guyton, JE Hall. Eleventh Edition

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - ClP1Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in the chloride (Cl-) pool or mass are a function of gains
and losses in chloride over time. Gains can occur from chloride in
the diet via reabsorption from the GI lumen; from intravenous
administration or from transfusion. Losses in chloride can occur in
urine; from sweating; from hemorrhage and during dialysis

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - ClP2Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Gain is defined as positive changes in mass of chloride
and is a function of dietary intake through GI lumen
absorption. Chloride can also be gained by intravenous drip
administration and/or intravenous blood transfusion.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialMass - ClP3Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The initial mass is the starting amount of chloride. Since the
plasma concentration is 106mEq/L (the Target[Cl-]) and the
volume of distribution is the ECFV (15,000mL), then the
initial mass or circulating pool is 1590.

Initial Mass = Target[Cl-]*ECFV.Vol

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - ClP4Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Loss is defined as negative changes in the mass of chloride. It
is a function of loss of chloride in the urine;loss in sweat; loss
during hemorrhage and loss during dialysis.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Mass - ClP5Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Mass is the calculated amount of chloride at any given time.
It is the change in chloride integrated over time.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Target[Cl-] - ClP6Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The Target[Cl-] is the initial plasma concentration of chloride.
Target[Cl-] = Initial Mass/ECFV.Vol

</content>
</topicdoc>
