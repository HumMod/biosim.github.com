<name> CreatineToCreatinine - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Creatinine is formed by nonenzymatic dehydration of
creatine and creatine phosphate within striated, smooth and
cardiac muscle. The rate of creatinine production in normal
subjects has been measured and was found to be 1.7% of the
creatine pool.

In HumMod, the creatine pool or mass is the sum of skeletal
muscle creatine mass and creatine in smooth & cardiac muscle,
collectively known as other mass.

Crim MC, Calloway DH, Margen S. Creatinine metabolism in men:
creatien pool size and turnover in relation to creatine intake.
J Nutr 106: 371 - 381, 1975.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> TotalMass - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

Skeletal muscle has a creatine concentration of
4mG/G Wet Weight. Since skeletal muscle mass is 40%
of body weight, skeletal muscle creatine mass is
calculated as 4.1 * 27 = 111G.

Other Mass = 17G

Total Mass = 17 + 111 = 128G

<topicdoc>
<name> K - DefinitionCC1 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

K is the rate constant for the conversion of creatine
to creatinine. Creatine exits muscle and is converted
to creatinine at a very steady rate constant of
0.0169/Day. Converting from G to mG/Min, this rate constant
becomes 0.0619 * 1000/1440 = 0.012. The units are (mG/Min)/G.

Since the molecular weight of creatine and creatinine are
131.15 and 113.12 respectively, the ratio is 0.86.

Therefore K = 0.012 * 0.86 = 0.010

Crim MC, Calloway DH, Margen S. Creatine metabolism in
men: urinary creatine and creatinine excretions with
creatine feeding. J. Nutr 106; 371 - 381, 1975.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> OtherMass - DefinitionCC2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

OtherMass is defined as the amount of creatine not
stored in skeletal muscle. It is approximately 10%
of total mass and has a value of 17G.

</content>
</topicdoc>
