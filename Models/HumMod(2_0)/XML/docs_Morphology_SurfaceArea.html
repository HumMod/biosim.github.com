<topicdoc>
<name> SurfaceArea - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Cardiac output increases approximately in proportion to
the surface area of the body. It is frequently expressed
as the cardiac index which is the cardiac output per
square meter of body surface area. It is important therefore
to be able to calculate body surface area based on height
and weight.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> SurfaceArea - Calculation </name>
<label> Calculation </label>
<content>

A = Area in cM^2
W = Weight in kG
H = Height in cM
C = Constant 71.84

Reduce C to 0.007184 to get area in M^2

A = C*H^0.725*W^0.425

DuBois D, DuBois EF A formula to estimate the
approximate surface area if height and weight
be known. Arch Int Med 17:863-871, 1916

surface area. J. Clin Anesth 4(1):4-10,1992

Haycock GB, Schwartz GJ, Wisotsky DH. Geometric
method for measuring body surface area: A height
weight formula validated in infants, children and
adults. The Journal of Pediatrics 93:1:62-66, 1978

Sendroy J, Cecchini LP. Determination of human body
surface area from height and weight.
J. Appl Physiol 7:1-12, 1954
