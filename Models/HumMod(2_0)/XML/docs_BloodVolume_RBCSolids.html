<topicdoc>
<name> RBCSolids - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Each red blood cell is comprised of water and solids,
the latter in the form of hemoglobin. Red cells have
the ability to concentrate hemoglobin in the cell fluid
up to 34grams in each 100mL of cells. In certain clinical
conditions (hypochromic anemia), inadequate quantities of
transferrin result in red cells that contain much less
hemoglobin than normal.

</content>
</topicdoc>
