<topicdoc>
<name> ReninTumor - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

A juxtaglomerular cell tumor of the kidney is a rare renal
neoplasm that was first described by Robertson et al in
1976. This tumor secretes large amounts of renin and patients
present with hypertension, hyperaldosteronism and hypokalamia.

Robertson PW, Klidjian A, Harding LK, Walters G, Lee MR,
Robb-Smith AH. Hypertension due to a renin-secreting
renal tumor. Am J Med 43: 963 - 976, 1967.

Conn JW, Cohen EL, Lucas CP, McDonald WJ, Mayor GH,
Blough WM Jr, Eveland WC, Bookstein JJ, Lapides J.
Primary reninism. Hypertension,hyperreninemia, and
secondary aldosteronism due to renin-producing
juxtaglomerular cell tumors.
Arch Intern Med 130: 682 - 696, 1972.

Orjavik OS, Fauchald P, Hovig T, Oystese B, Brodwall EK
Renin-secreting renal tumor with severe hypertension. case
report with tumor renin analysis, histpathological and
ultrastructural studies. Acta Med Scand 197: 329 - 335, 1975.

Baruch D, Corvol P, Alhenc-Gelas F, Dufloux MA, Guyenne TT,
Gaux JC, Raynaud A, Brisset JM, Duclos JM, Menard J.
Diagnosis and traetment of renin-secreting tumors. Report
of three cases. Hypertension 6: 760 - 766, 1984.

Martin SA, Mynderse LA, Lager DJ, Cheville JC.
Juxtaglomerular cell tumor: a clinicopathologic study of
four cases and review of the literature.
Am J Clin Pathol 116: 854 - 863, 2001.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Setting - DefinitionRT1 </name>
<label> Definition</label>
<content>

The model user can create a renin - secreting tumor by turning
the switch "on" in the Circulating Renin - Tumor display box.
Rates of renin secretion can be set from 0 GU/Min up to 100
GU/Min in increments of 10; from 100 to 1000 GU/MIn in increments
of 100 and from 1000 to 10,000 GU/Min in increments of 1000.
This is achieved using the sliding bar feature in the display
box.

</content>
</topicdoc>
