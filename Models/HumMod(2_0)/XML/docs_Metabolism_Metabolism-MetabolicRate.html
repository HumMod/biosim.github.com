<topicdoc>
<name> Metabolic Rate - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Energy requirements are determined by body size and composition,
age and physical activity. Total energy expenditure consists of
basal metabolic rate, which accounts for 50 -70% of the total in
an average adult, plus variable components due to physical activity
and thermogenesis.
The main determinant of resting metabolic rate (BMR) is the mass
of metabolically active tissue, of which lean, fat-free tissue is
the most important.

Kruszynska YT. Normal metabolism: the physiology of fuel
homeostasis. Textbook of Diabetes. Chapter 9. Edited by
Pickup JC & Willams G. Third Edition 2003. Blackwell Science.

Ravussin E, Burnand B, Schutz Y, Jequier E. Twenty-four-hour
energy expenditure and resting metabolic rate in obese,
moderately obese, and control subjects.
Am J Clin Nutr 35:566 -573, 1982.
