<topicdoc>
<name> SID - DefinitionBI1 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Peter Stewart was a Canadian physiologist who introduced
the term "strong ion difference" or [SID] to mean the
concentration of strongly dissociating cations minus
the concentration of strongly dissociating anions. Strong
cations are Na+, K+ and strong anions are Cl- and lactate.

The concentrations of anions and cations in both plasma
and cells must be equal to maintain electrical neutrality.

His equation is:

[SID] + [H+] = [HCO3-] + [A-] + [CO2] + [OH-]

where [A-] is the concentration of dissociated weak acids.

In HumMod, SID is calculated from the cations [Na+] and [K+].
The anions used are [Cl-], [Lac-]. Additionally the following
strong anions are included: Ketoacids [KA-], [SO4--] and [PO--].
Under normal physiological conditions the contribution of the
latter three anions is small.

Stewart PA. Independent and dependent variables of acid-base
control. Respir Physiol 33: 9�26, 1978.

Stewart PA. How to Understand Acid-Based: A Quantitative
Acid-Base Primer for Biology and Medicine. New York: Elsevier, 1981.

Stewart PA. Modern quantitative acid-base chemistry.
Can J Physiol Pharmacol 61: 1444�1461, 1983.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Anions - DefinitionBI2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Many substances dissociate into ions when dissolved
in water. Such substances are called electrolytes.
Negatively charged electrolytes are called anions and
can be divided into strong and weak anions. Strong
anions are always completely dissociated in solution
and the resulting anions are considered strong anions.
Weak anions are only partially dissociated into ions
when they are dissolved in water.

Strong anions are chloride (Cl-), ketoacids (KA-),
lactate (Lac-), sulphate (SO4--) and phosphate (PO --).
Bicarbonate (HCO3-) is a weak anion. Proteins are considered
weak anions because of their negative electric charge.

Stewart PA. Independent and dependent variables of acid-base
control. Respir Physiol 33: 9�26, 1978.

Stewart PA. How to Understand Acid-Based: A Quantitative
Acid-Base Primer for Biology and Medicine. New York: Elsevier, 1981.

Stewart PA. Modern quantitative acid-base chemistry.
Can J Physiol Pharmacol 61: 1444�1461, 1983.

Figge J, Mydosh T, Fencl V. Serum proteins and acid -base

<topicdoc>
<name> AnionsLessProtein - DefinitionBI3 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Proteins in the body function as bases and have
negative charges that readily accept hydrogen ions.
Proteins are considered weak anions.

The concentrations of anions and cations in both plasma
and cells must be equal to maintain electrical neutrality.

Total Cation Concentration = Total Anion Concentration
= 148.5 mEq/L

Total Anion Concentration = Weak + Strong Anions + Protein
Total (148.5 mEq/L) = Weak Anions (28 mEq/L) + Strong Anions (108.5
mEq/L) + Protein (12.0 mEq/L)

Anions Less Protein = Weak + Strong Anions
148.5 mEq/L - 12.0 mEq/L = 28.0 mEq/L + 108.5 mEq/L

Figge J, Rossing TH, Fencl V. The role of serum proteins in
acid-base equilibria. J. Lab Clin Med 117: 453 - 467, 1991.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Cations - DefinitionBI4 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Many substances dissociate into ions when dissolved
in water. Such substances are called electrolytes.
Positively charged electrolytes are called cations
Strong electrolytes are always completely dissociated
in solution and the resulting ions are considered
strong cations.

in HumMod, cations are sodium [Na+], potassium [K+]
and magnesium

Normal values are 144 mEq/L for sodium and 4.4 mEq/L
for potassium.


</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Protein - DefinitionBI6 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Proteins in the body function as bases. They have negative
charges that readily accept hydrogen ions. Together with
weak anions and strong anions they contribute to the total
anion concentration.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> StrongAnions - DefinitionBI7 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Negatively charged anions which completely dissociate in
solution are known as strong anions. Strong anions are
chloride, ketoacids, lactate, phosphate and sulphate.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> WeakAnions - DefinitionBI8 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Negatively charged electrolytes which only partially
dissociate in solution are called weak anions. Bicarbonate
is a weak anion.

</content>
</topicdoc>
