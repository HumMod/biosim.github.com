HepaticArty.BasicConductance

Definition

Conductance is a measure of the blood flow through
a vessel for a given pressure difference. It is the
exact reciprocal of resistance. For the hepatic artery
the pressure gradient is the difference between
systemic arterial pressure and splanchnic venous pressure.
Basic Conductance is conductance when the viscosity is 1.

Physical Units

Flow is expressed in mL/min
Conductance is expressed as (mL/Min)/mmHg
Pressure gradient in mmHg

Concentration Units

Typical values for hepatic artery blood flow is
250mL/min. Arterial pressure is 97mmHg and splanchnic
venous pressure 8mmHg for a pressure gradient of 89mmHg
Typical conductance units for the hepatic artery are
2.8(mL/Min)/mmHg
