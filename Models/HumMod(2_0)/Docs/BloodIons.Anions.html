BloodIons.Anions

Definition

Many substances dissociate into ions when dissolved
in water. Such substances are called electrolytes.
Negatively charged electrolytes are called anions and
can be divided into strong and weak anions. Strong
anions are always completely dissociated in solution
and the resulting anions are considered strong anions.
Weak anions are only partially dissociated into ions
when they are dissolved in water.

Strong anions are chloride (Cl-), ketoacids (KA-),
lactate (Lac-), sulphate (SO4--) and phosphate (PO --).
Bicarbonate (HCO3-) is a weak anion. Proteins are considered
weak anions because of their negative electric charge.
