AldoPump.Setting

Defintion

When the AldoPump switch is turned on, the model
user can set infusion rates using the sliding bar
feature in the ADH pump display box. Rates of infusion
may be increased from 0, in increments of 10, up to
4000 pMol/Min.

Normal adrenal aldosterone secretion rate is 330.0 pMol/Min.

Overview

The AldoPump enables the model user to change
[Aldo] levels by infusing aldosterone at different
rates. Data is taken from a variety of human and
animal studies relating infusion rates to plasma
concentrations of Aldosterone

When the switch is turned on, the model user sets the
rate by changing the setting using the slide bar.

Dog Data
WF Ganong, PJ Mulrow. Rate of change in sodium and
potassium excretion after injection of aldosterone
into the aorta and renal artery of the dog.
Am J Physiol 195: 337 - 342, 1958.

Pan YJ, Young DB. Experimental aldosterone hypertension
in the dog. Hypertension 4: 279 - 287, 1982.

Physical Units

Aldo concentrations have been expressed in nG/100ml
and pMol/L.
1pMol = 360.4pG = 0.3604nG
1pMol/L = 0.36nG/L = 0.036nG/100ml
1nG/100mL = 27.75pMol/L
12 nG/100mL = 333pMol/L

Concentration Units

A typical [Aldo]concentration is 330pMol/L or 12nG/dL.
Molecular weight of aldosterone is 360.45.
Thus 1 pMol = 360.45 pG.
