GlucagonPool.[Glucagon]

Overview

Glucagon is a single long-chain polypeptide secreted
by the alpha cells of the pancreatic islets of Langerhans.
It plays an important role in glucose homeostasis.
It is released into the hepatic portal circulation in
response to decreases in plasma glucose levels.
Glucagon stimulates gluconeogenesis and glycogenolysis
in the liver. Both lead to an enhanced availability of
glucose. At the same time, it decreases glycogenesis
and glycolysis

Glucagon is the main counterregulatory hormone to insulin
and the insulin to glucagon ratio determines the control
of gluconeogenesis and glycogenolysis. Glucagon increases
the hepatic uptake of precursors, especially amino acids,
and their conversion into glucose.

Physical Units

Plasma Concentration pG/mL; pMol/L
1 pG/mL = 0.282 pMol/L
Mass pG or nG
Secretion nG/Min

Concentration Units

Molecular Weight (MW) of glucagon is 3550

Typical plasma glucagon concentration [Glucagon] = 70 pG/mL
The volume of distribution is the ECFV.

Glucagon Mass = ECFV * [Glucagon]
= 15000 * 70 = 1,050,000 pG or 1,050 nG

Typical glucagon concentrations under a variety of
physiological situations


Starve 300pG/mL
Fast 100pG/mL
Drip 70pG/mL
Meal 50pG/mL
Load 40pG/mL
