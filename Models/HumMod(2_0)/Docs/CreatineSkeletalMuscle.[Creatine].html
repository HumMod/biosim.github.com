CreatineSkeletalMuscle.[Creatine]

Overview

Creatine in the body arises from two sources:
endogenous synthesis from amino acids and creatine
in the diet, notably from the ingestion of meat.
Endogenous synthesis occurs primarily in the kidney
but also occurs in the liver. Creatine is concentrated
and stored mainly in skeletal muscle where it is used
as an energy source, particularly during strenous exercise
when oxidative metabolism is too slow to provide sufficient
energy. 90% of the body stores of creatine are contained in
skeletal muscle. The remaining 10% is contained in smooth
and cardiac muscle. Creatine leaks into the extracellular
fluid where it is converted to creatinine.

Creatinine is excreted both renally and extrarenally.
Extrarenal degradation probably occurs in the intestine
by intestinal bacteria. Extrarenal excretion is about 1.5%
of total excretion and varies with plasma creatinine
concentration.

Definition

[Creatine] is the concentration of creatine in skeletal
muscle expressed as mG/G of muscle mass

Physical Units

Units for mass are G
Units for [Ceatine] are mG/G

Concentration Units

Skeletal muscle has a creatine concentration of
4mG/G Wet Weight. Since skeletal muscle mass is 40%
of body weight, skeletal muscle creatine mass is
calculated as 4.1 * 27 = 111G.

Assumptions: Initial body weight = 72KG
Muscle Mass = 27.7KG
