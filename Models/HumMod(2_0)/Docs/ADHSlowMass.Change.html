ADHSlowMass.Change

Overview

In HumMod, the amount of ADH stored in the posterior pituitary is
divided into two distict masses - the SlowMass and the FastMass.
This assumption comes from hemorrhage studies in dogs which show
that the initial secretory response is not sustained in the
presence of a maintained stimulus of hemorrhagic hypotension.

When a chronic stimulus is applied, sizeable depletion of hormonal
stores occurs and a coincidental increase in synthesis occurs. This
is the Slow Mass of ADH and is the predominant factor controlling
rate of synthesis in the hypothalamus. In HumMod, feedback is a multiplier
of base secretion and is dependant on the Slow Mass storage level.
As the Slow Mass levels decrease, synthesis increases.

Sachs H, Share L, Osinchak J, Carpi A. Capacity of the neurohypophysis
to release vasopressin. Endocrinology 81: 755 - 770, 1967.

Definition

Change in the SlowMass pool can occur in three ways:
1. ADH synthesis increases the Slow Mass pool
2. Flux of ADH out of the SlowMass pool into the FastMass pool
depletes the FastMass pool.
3. Flux of ADH out of FastMass pool into the SlowMass pool increases
the SlowMass pool.

Change is therefore defined as
ADHSynthesis.Rate + ADHFastMass.Flux - ADHSlowMass.Flux
