NESecretion.Base

Definition

Base secretion is the rate of secretion from the adrenal
medullae when the adrenal nerve effect is 1.0.

Goldstein DS, Eisenhofer G, Kopin IJ. Sources and significance
of plasma levels of catechols and their metabolites in humans.
J Pharmacol Exp Ther 305(3): 800-811, 2003.

Overview

Norepinephrine (NE) is the neurotransmitter released
from axon terminals of sympathetic postganglionic neurons.
Norepinephrine is also released from the adrenal medullae
in response to sympathetic nerve stimulation. Increases
in adrenal nerve activity cause increases in the release
of NE from both nerve endings and from the adrenal medullae.
Under resting conditions, only a few percent of the NE
entering the plasma represents adrenal medullary secretion.
The remainder has its source in the neurotransmitter released
by sympathetic nerve varicosities. This NE is referred to
as spillover. Total body spillover can be measured using a
tracer kinetic method based on dilution of [3H]NE by endogenous NE.
During stress responses e.g. acute glucodeprivation, the adrenal
contribution to plasma NE levels greatly increases.

In the model, the adrenal nerve effect is quantified as a
multiplier of the base secretion.

NE excites both alpha and beta adrenergic receptors and causes
a range of different effects in different effector organs
depending on the plasma concentration and the density and types
of receptors present. In patients with pheochromocytoma, an
increase in plasma NE causes symptoms which include
hypertension, severe headaches, palpitations and excess sweating.
These symptoms reflect the effects of norepinephrine
on alpha and beta adrenergic receptors respectively.
