ANPPool.[ANP(pG/mL)]

Overview

Atrial natriuretic peptide (ANP) is also known as atrial
natriuretic factor(ANF) and atriopeptin. ANP is a potent
natriuretic peptide involved in hormonal regulation of
extracellular fluid volume and blood pressure homeostasis.
It is secreted by the atrial walls of the heart
in response to stretch, which can occur from excess blood
volume. ANP acts on the kidneys to cause small increases in
glomerular filtration rate (GFR) and decreases in in sodium
reabsorption by the collecting ducts.

Definition

[ANP(pG/mL)] is the plasma concentration of ANP expressed
in pG/mL.

Concentration Units

ANP is a 28 amino acid protein with a
molecular weight of 3060

A typical concentration is 20pMol/L
1pMol/L = 3pG/mL

Normal ANP Secretion Rate is 67pMol/min.
Clearance Rate is 67 pMol/Min
Right Heart Secretion Rate: 41pMol/Min and
Left Heart Secretion Rate: 26pMol/min
