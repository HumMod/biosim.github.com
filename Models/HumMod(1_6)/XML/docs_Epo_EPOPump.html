<topicdoc>
<name> EPOPump - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The EPO Pump enables the model user to change
[EPO] levels by infusing EPO at different rates.
The switch enables the model user to turn the
pump on; the setting enables a particular rate of
infusion.

The anemia of chronic renal disease is often a
result of a lack of erythropoietin. Administration
of EPO using the pump illustrates the beneficial
effects of EPO in those patients.

Data is taken from a variety of human and animal
studies relating infusion rates to plasma
concentrations of EPO.

Human Data:
Eschbach JW,Kelly MR, Haley NR, Abels RI, Adamson JW
Treatment of the anemia of progressive renal failure
with recombinant human erythropoetin.
New Engl J Medicine 321:158-163, 1989

Winearls CG, Oliver DO, Pippard MJ, Reid C,
Downing MR, Cotes PM. Effect of human erythropoietin
derived from recombinant DNA on the anaemia of patients
maintained by chronic haemodialysis.
Lancet 2:1175-1178, 1986

Rat Data
Reissmann KR, Diederich DA, Ito K, Schmans JW.
Influence of disappearance rate and distribution
space on plasma concentration of erthropoietin
in normal rats. J Lab Clin Med 65:967-975, 1965

<topicdoc>
<name> EPO - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

EPO mass is typically 136 U with a
volume of distribution of 40% of
ECFV, yielding a typical conc of
22 mU/ml.[EPO] levels range from 10-30mU/mL
in humans.Typical secretion and destruction
rates are 0.75 U/Min.


Plasma Levels in Humans

at high altitude and its relation to plasma renin.
J Appl Physiology 59: 360-364, 1985

Plasma levels of immunoreactive erythropoietin
after acute blood loss in man.

3.Kokot M, Kokot F, Franek E, Wiecek A, Nowicki M, Dulawa J
Effect of Isobaric hyperoxemia on erythropoietin secretion
