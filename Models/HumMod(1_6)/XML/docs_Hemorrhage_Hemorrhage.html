<topicdoc>
<name> Hemorrhage - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Circulatory shock means generalized inadequate blood flow
through the body. Shock usually results from inadequate
cardiac output. Factors that decrease venous return lead
to a decrease in cardiac output and cause circulatory shock.
The most common cause of decreased venous return is a diminished
blood volume. In the model, the user can create hypovolemic
shock by causing a hemorrhage. The volume and rate of blood loss
can be set by the user.

Adamson J, Hillman RS. Blood Volume and plasma protein
replacement following acute blood loss in normal man.
J. Amer.Med.Assoc. 205:609-612, 1968.

Ebert RV, Stead Jr. EA,Gibson II JG. Response of normal
subjects to acute blood loss.
Arch.Int.Med. 68:578-590, 1941.
