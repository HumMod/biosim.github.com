<topicdoc>
<name> PlasmaVol - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

About 60% of the blood volume is plasma.
In the average adult male (70Kg) this is
approximately 3 Liters. This percentage varies
considerably depending on gender, weight, and
other factors.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - PV1Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in plasma volume are defined by three variables:
The intial volume and the gain and loss of plasma volume.

</content>
</topicdoc>


<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - PV2Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Gains in plasma volume may result from changes in
gut absorption; the peritoneal lymph system;
the systemic lymphatic system;the pulmonary lymphatic
system. Gains can also occur by intravenous
administration and blood transfusion.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialVol - PV3Definition </name>
<label> Defintion </label>
<content>

Initial volume is the starting plasma volume.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - PV4Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Loss is defined as negative changes in plasma volume.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Vol - PV5Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Vol is the calcualted plasma volume at any given time.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Vol(L) - PV6Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Vol is the calculated plasma volume at any given time.
Vol(L) is the plasma volume expressed in Liters
Vol(L) = Vol/1000.00

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> PlasmaVol.Loss - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Loss in plasma volume may occur in several
different areas:excretion by the kidney;
the peritoneum space;the capillaries;
the pulmonary system. Loss can also occur as
a result of hemorrhage and hemodialysis.

</content>
</topicdoc>
