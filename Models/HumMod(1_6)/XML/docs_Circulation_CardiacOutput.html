<topicdoc>
<name> CardiacOutput - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Cardiac Output is the quantity of blood pumped into the
aorta each minute by the heart.
Cardiac Ouput = heart rate*stroke volume.
Cardiac Index is cardiac output expressed as a
function of body surface area

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> CardiacOutput - Physical Units </name>
<label> Physical Units </label>
<content>

Cardiac output may be expressed in mL/Min or as
a function of body surface area.
Cardiac Index mL/min/m^2

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> StrokeVolume - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Stroke volume(SV) is the difference between
end-diastolic volume(EDV) and the end-systolic
volume(ESV). EDV is the filled volume of the
left ventricle prior to contraction and the
ESV is the residual volume of blood remaining
after ejection.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> StrokeVolume - Normal Values </name>
<label> Normal Values </label>
<content>

In a typical heart, the EDV is about 120mL
of blood and the ESV about 50mL of blood.
The difference of these two volumes, 70mL,
represents the SV.

</content>
</topicdoc>
