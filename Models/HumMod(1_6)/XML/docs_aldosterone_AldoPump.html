<topicdoc>
<name> AldoPump - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The Aldo Pump enables the model user to change aldosterone
levels by infusing aldosterone at different rates. The model
user first turns the switch on to activate the pump and then
sets the rate of infusion.

Data is taken from a variety of human and animal studies
relating infusion rates to plasma concentrations of aldosterone.

WF Ganong, PJ Mulrow. Rate of change in sodium and potassium excretion
after injection of aldosterone into the aorta and renal artery of the dog.
Am J Physiol 195: 337 - 342, 1958.

Pan YJ, Young DB. Experimental aldosterone hypertension in the dog.
Hypertension 4: 279 - 287, 1982.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Aldo - Physical Units</name>
<label> Physical Units </label>
<content>

Aldo concentrations have been expressed in nG/100ml
and pMol/L.
nG/dL = 0.036 * pMol/L.
Aldosterone pump rate units are pMol/Min

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Aldo - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

A typical [Aldo] concentration is 330pMol/L.
Molecular weight of aldosterone is 360.45. Thus 1 pMol = 360.45 pG.

The Merck Index. 12th Edition 1996

</content>
