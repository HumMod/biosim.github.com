<topicdoc>
<name> ArtysVol - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The blood volume contained within the arterial side
of the circulation is the sum of the volumes contained
within various arterial compartments. These compartments
are:pulmonary capillaries, pulmonary Veins, the left atrium,
the left ventricle, systemic arteries and the sequestered
arterial volume.

Leggett RW, Williams LR. Suggested reference values for
regional blood volumes in humans.
Health Physics 60(2):139-154, 1991.

Leggett RW, Williams LR. A proposed blood circulation model
for reference man. Health Physics 69(2):187-201; 1995

For reference about sequestered blood volume see:
Thornton WE, Moore TP, Pool SL. Fluid Shifts in Weightlessness
Aviat. Space Environ Med 58(9, Suppl.):A86-90, 1987

Moore TP, Thornton WE Space shuttle inflight and postflight
fluid shifts measured by leg volume changes. Aviat Space
Environ. Med 58(9, Suppl.):A91-96, 1987
