<topicdoc>
<name> PeripheralFlow - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Peripheral flow is all organ flow minus the
Hepatic Vein Flow.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> PeripheralFlow - Physical Units </name>
<label> Physical Units </label>
<content>

Flow is expressed in mL/min

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> PeripheralFlow - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

Typical values for peripheral flow are 4180mL/Min

Individual organ flows are shown below :

Organ Flow (mL/Min)
-------- -------
Bone 420
Brain 820
Fat 240
Kidney 1200
LHeart 190
Other 380
RespMus 100
RHeart 40
SkeMus 650
Skin 140
------- -------
Total 4180

Williams LR and Leggett RW. Reference values for resting
blood flow to organs of man. Clinical Physics and
Physiological Measurement Vol 10, No 3, 187 - 217;1989.

Leggett RW and Williams LR. A proposed blood circulation
model for reference man. Health Physics 69(2):187-201;1995

</content>
</topicdoc>

Total 1250mL/min
