CardiacOutput.StrokeVolume

Overview

Stroke volume(SV) is the difference between
end-diastolic volume(EDV) and the end-systolic
volume(ESV). EDV is the filled volume of the
left ventricle prior to contraction and the
ESV is the residual volume of blood remaining
after ejection.

Normal Values

In a typical heart, the EDV is about 120mL
of blood and the ESV about 50mL of blood.
The difference of these two volumes, 70mL,
represents the SV.
