HgbProps.HILLCONSTANT

Definition

HILLCONSTANT has a value of 2.3

HILLCONSTANT is a exponent used in the equation
relating blood oxygen content and blood pO2.
The relationship is described using the sigmoidal
Hill equation:

S = An / 1 + An

where S is the fraction saturation, An is
(PO2SATURATED/P50)^HILLCONSTANT where
PO2SATURATED is the pO2 at 100% saturation
and p50 is the pO2 that produces a saturation
of 1/2 or 50%.

Dill DB, Edwards HT, Florkin M, Campbell RW.
Properties of dog blood.
J Biol. Chem. 95: 143 - 152, 1932.

Hill AV. The possible effects of the aggregation of
the molecules of haemoglobin on its dissociation curves.
J Physiol. 40: iv - vii, 1910.
