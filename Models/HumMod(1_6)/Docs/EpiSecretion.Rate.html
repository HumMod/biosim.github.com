EpiSecretion.Rate

Definition

Epinephrine secretion rate is calculated from the
base rate of secretion * Adrenal nerve effect. Additionally,
since control of secretion is a function of membrane transport,
the effect of ph, protein, fuel and temperature are important
factors. These are classified as other tissue-function and act
as a multiplier effect. Under normal physiological conditions,
they have a value of 1.0.

Overview

Epinephrine is secreted by the chromaffin cells of the
adrenal medulla in response to sympathetic stimulation.
Increases in adrenal nerve activity result in an increase
in secretion.

Physical Units

Secretion rates are expressed in nG/min
Adrenal nerve effect is quantified in multiples of 1.0

Concentration Units

Typical base secretion rate is 375nG/Min.
Rate is calculated as a function of base secretion and
adrenal nerve effect.

Under normal conditions, efferent adrenal nerve activity
is 1.5 Hz. This level of activity is an adrenal nerve
effect of 1.0.
