Overview

Insulin has a plasma half life of about 6 minutes.
Except for the portion of insulin which binds with
receptors in target cells, the remainder is degraded
by the enzyme insulinase mainly in the liver, to a
lesser extent in the kidneys and muscles and slightly
in most other tissues.
