Overview

Primary aldosteronism or "Conn's syndrome" occurs when a
small tumor of the zona glomerulosa cells develops and
secretes large amounts of aldosterone. The most important
effects are hypokalemia, a slight increase in extracellular
fluid volume and blood volume, a very slight increase in
plasma sodium concentration and hypertension.

Patients with primary aldosteronism often exhibit occasional
periods of muscle paralysis caused by hypokalemia. The
paralysis is caused by a depressant effect of low extracellular
potassium concentration on action potential transmission by
nerve fibers.

The model user can set secretion rates of an aldosterone
secreting tumor. Normal secretion rate of aldosterone
is 330.0 pMol/Min
