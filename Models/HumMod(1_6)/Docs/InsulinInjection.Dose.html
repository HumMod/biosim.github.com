InsulinInjection.Dose

Overview

Insulin can be administered in two ways for the treatment
of diabetes. The first is by multiple daily injections
(MDI); the second by continuous subcutaneous insulin
infusion. In the model, the latter method of administration
is the InsulinPump.

Multiple daily injections (MDI) occurs under the structure
name "Insulin Injection" and is not yet functional in the
current model. Diabetic patients usually inject four or more
times daily using a combination of short- and long-acting
insulins. Injections are given subcutaneously

Physical Units

Units for insulin pool are mU.
Units for [plasma] are uU/mL.

An international unit (U) is
1/24 mG.
Molecular weight of Insulin is 5754.

uU * 0.04167 = nG
uU * 0.007241 = pMol

Concentration Units

A typical maintenance dose of insulin is 40U/day
The insulin can have three different durations of action:
rapid (4 hours); intermediate (10 hours) and long acting
(18 hours). This is InsulinInjection.Type.
