CarotidSinus.Switch

Overview

The carotid sinus is a region located on the wall
of each internal carotid artery slightly above the
the carotid bifurcation of the carotid artery.
It contains abundant pressure receptors called
baroreceptors. These baroreceptors are spray-type
nerve endings that are stimulated when stretched.
Signals are transmitted from each each carotid sinus
through very small Hering's nerve to the glossopharyngeal
nerve and then to the tractus solitarius in the medullary
area of the brain stem. The baroreceptors respond
extremely rapidly to changes in arterial pressure.
Excitation reflexly causes the arterial pressure to
decrease by decreasing both peripheral resistance and
cardiac output.

The switch feature enables the model user to disable
the carotid sinus reflex. When the switch is off,
carotid artery pressure is equal to arterial pressure
and the reflex is functional. When the switch is on,
the carotid artery pressure is equal to a fixed perfusion
pressure defined by the user.

vasomotor responses to separate and combined stimulation
of carotid sinus and aortic arch baroreceptors by
pulsatile and non-pulsatile pressures in the dog.
