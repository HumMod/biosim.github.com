<topicdoc>
<name> ANP - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Atrial natriuretic peptide (ANP) is also known as atrial
natriuretic factor(ANF) and atriopeptin. ANP is a potent
natriuretic peptide involved in hormonal regulation of
extracellular fluid volume and blood pressure homeostasis.
It is secreted by the atrial walls of the heart
in response to stretch, which can occur from excess blood
volume. ANP acts on the kidneys to cause small increases in
glomerular filtration rate (GFR) and decreases in in sodium
reabsorption by the collecting ducts.

Reviews
Ruskoaho H. Atrial natriuretic peptide: synthesis, release
and metabolism. Pharmacological Reviews 44: 479 - 602, 1992.

de Zeeuw D, Janssen WM, de Jong PE. Atrial natriuretic
Factor: Its (patho)physiological significance in humans.
Kidney International 41: 1115 - 1133, 1992.


</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> [ANP] - DefinitionANPP2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

[ANP] is the plasma concentration of ANP. If the model
user has switched the clamp on, then [ANP] has the value set
by the sliding bar in the ANPPool display box. Otherwise
[ANP] = ANPPool.Mass/ECFV.Vol(L)

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - DefinitionANPP3 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in the ANP pool or mass are a function of both
gains and losses in ANP over time. Gains may occur from
increased secretion and from infusion of ANP using the
ANP pump feature of the model. Loss occurs from ANP
clearance from the blood

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Clamp - DefinitionANPP4 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The Clamp enables the model user to fix ANP plasma levels.
The model user turns the clamp to "on" in the ANP Pool
display box. The secretion level is set by using the
sliding bar. Secretion rates can be set from zero pMol/L
up to a maximum of 100 pMol/Min in increments of 1.


</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Derv - DefinitionANPP5 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Derv is the derivative of the Backward Euler for
calculation of the mass of ANP.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> F1 - DefinitionANPP6 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

F1 is a mathematical variable used in the calculation of
the mass of ANP. F1 is the gain in ANP over time and is
a function of ANP secretion rate and ANP Pump Rate.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> F2 - DefinitionANPP7 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

F2 is a mathematical variable used in the calculation of
the mass of ANP. F2 is the loss in ANP mass over time
and is a function of ANP clearance rate.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - DefinitionANPP8 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Gain is defined as positive changes in mass of ANP
and is a function of ANP secretion rate, and
ANP from infusion using the ANP pump feature.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialMass - DefinitionANPP9 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The initial mass or pool is the Target[ANP]
multiplied by the initial volume of distribution, in
this case the ECFV.

Initial Mass = Target[ANP]*ECFV.InitialVol(L)

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Level - DefinitionANPP10 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The model user may fix ANP plasma concentration at a
particular level by switching the clamp to "on" in the
ANP pool display box. The concentration level is then set
using the sliding bar. Plasma concentration can be set
anywhere from zero pMol/L up to a maximum of 100 pMol/L
in increments of 1.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Log10Conc - DefinitionANPP11 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Log10Conc calculates the logarithm of the plasma
concentration of ANP. If [ANP] is less than 1.0,
then Log10Conc has a value of zero.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - DefinitionANPP12 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Loss is defined as a negative change in mass of ANP
and is a function of clearance rate.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Mass - DefinitionANPP13 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Mass is the calculated amount of ANP at any
any given time. Change in the ANP pool or mass is a
function of both gains and losses in ANP over time.
Gains may occur from increases in ANP secretion rate,
from infusion of ANP using the ANP pump feature of HumMod.
Changes in the pool are calculated in the model using two
mathematical variables, F1 and F2, which integrate gains and
losses over time. A Backward Euler integral is used in this
calculation because it gives a more stable solution set.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<name> PMOLTOPG - DefinitionANPP14 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

PMOLTOPG is a constant used to convert pMol to pG
PMOLTOPG = 3.060
1pMol = 3.060pG

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Target[ANP] - DefinitionANPP15 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

During startup, the model user defines the particular
traits of the HumMod patient. These are gender, body size,
age, muscularity and adiposity. All of these traits
influence body volumes, most notably the extracellular
fluid volume (ECFV). The assumption is made that
while volumes may change with traits, concentrations do
not. At startup,initial volumes,especially ECFV and plasma
volume are calculated. A target concentration is specified
- in this case Target[ANP].

The initial mass or circulating pool is the Target[ANP]
multiplied by the initial volume of distribution, in
this case the ECFV.

Initial Mass = Target[ANP]*ECFV.InitialVol(L)

</content>
</topicdoc>
