<name> RBCH2O - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Each red blood cell is comprised of water and solids,
the latter in the form of hemoglobin.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - RBCH2O1Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Change in the water content of the red blood cell is the
difference between gains and losses in red cell water over
time.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> FractH2O - RBCH2O2Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

FractH2O is the amount of water contained in the red cell.
It is expressed as a fraction of the total volume of the
red cell.
FractH2O = 1.0 - RBCSolids.FractSolids.
FractH2O is set by the model user by changing the parameter
FractSolids using a sliding bar in HumMod. As the amount of
solids in the red cell increase, the amount of H2O decreases.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - RBCH2O3Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Gains in red blood cell H2O volume are a result
of either a decrease in fraction of the cell which
is comprised of solids (RBCSolids.FractSolids) and/or
a gain in the total volume of red blood cells from
either increased secretion rate and/or blood
transfusion.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialVol - RBCH2O4Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The initial H2O volume of the red cell is a function of the
parameter RBCSolids.FractSolids and hence the varaible
RBCH2O.FractH2O as well as the initial volume of red blood
cells (RBCVol.InitialVol)

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - RBCH2O5Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Loss in red blood cell water content is a result of an
increase in the fraction of the red cell which is
comprised of solids (RBCSolids. FractSolids) as well
as a loss in total red cells due to increased clearance
and/or hemorrhage.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Vol - RBCH2O6Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Red cell water volume is calculated from the initial
volume and the change in volume integrated over time.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Vol(L) - RBCH2O7Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Vol(L) is the calculated volume expressed in liters
Vol(L) = Vol/1000.00

</content>
</topicdoc>
