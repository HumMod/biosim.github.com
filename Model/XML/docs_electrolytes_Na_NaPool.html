<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The extracellular fluid contains large amounts of sodium ions
and plasma sodium is normally regulated within very narrow limits.

Textbook of Medical Physiology AC Guyton, JE Hall. Eleventh Edition

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - NaP1Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in the sodium (Na+) pool or mass are a function of gains
and losses in sodium over time. Gains can occur from sodium in
the diet via reabsorption from the GI lumen; from intravenous
administration or from transfusion. Losses in sodium can occur in
urine; from sweating; from hemorrhage and during dialysis

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - NaP2Definition </name>
<label> Defintion </label>
<content>

Gain is defined as positive changes in mass of sodium
and is a function of dietary intake through GI lumen
absorption. Sodium can also be gained by intravenous drip
administration and/or intravenous blood transfusion.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialMass - NaP3Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The initial mass is the starting amount of sodium. Since the
plasma concentration is 144mEq/L (the Target[Na+]) and the
volume of distribution is the ECFV (15,000mL), then the
initial mass or circulating pool is 2160.

Initial Mass = Target[Na+]*ECFV.Vol

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - NaP4Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Loss is defined as negative changes in the mass of sodium. It
is a function of loss of sodium in the urine;loss in sweat; loss
during hemorrhage and loss during dialysis.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Mass - NaP5Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Mass is the calculated amount of sodium at any given time.
It is the change in sodium integrated over time.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Target[Na+] - NaP6Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The Target[Na+] is the initial plasma concentration of sodium.
Target[Na+] = Initial Mass/ECFV.Vol

</content>
</topicdoc>
