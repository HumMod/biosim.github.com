<topicdoc>
<name> BMI -Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The body mass index (BMI), or Quetelet index is a statistical
measurement which compares a person's weight and height and
is used as a diagnostic tool to identify weight problems within
a population. It was invented between 1830 and 1850 by the
Belgian Adolphe Quetelet. BMI is defined as the individual's body
weight divided by the square of his or her height.

Physique Sociale 2:92, 1869.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> BMI-Values </name>
<label> Values </label>
<content>

BMI = W / H^2

BMI = Body Mass Index in kG M^2
W = Weight in kG
H = Height in M

Typical values are

BMI = 24.5 kG/M^2
W = 77.5 kG
H = 1.78 M
