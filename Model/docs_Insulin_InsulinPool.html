<topicdoc>
<name> Insulin - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Insulin was first isolated from the pancreas in 1922 by
Banting and Best. It is synthesized in and secreted by
the beta cells of the Islets of Langerhans in the pancreas
and plays an important role in the regulation of glucose,
lipid and protein metabolism. Insulin secretion is associated
with energy abundance. When there is a great abundance of
energy-giving foods in the diet, especially excess amounts
of carbohydrates, insulin is secreted in great quantity.
In turn, the insulin plays an important role in storing
the excess energy. The primary controller of insulin
secretion is increased blood glucose concentration.
Insulin increases the transport of glucose into liver,
muscle and other cells, thereby reducing the blood
glucose concentration back towards a normal value.
Other factors which stimulate insulin secretion include
increased blood amino acids; gastrointestinal hormones
including gastrin, secretin, cholecystokinin and gastric
inhibitory peptide.

Guyton AC & Hall JE Textbook of medical physiology.
Eleventh Edition. Elsevier Inc


</content>
</topicdoc>

<name> Insulin - Physical Units </name>
<label> Physical Units </label>
<content>

Units for insulin pool are mU.
Units for [plasma] are uU/mL.
Uptake - mG/Min

An international unit (U) is 1/24 mG.
Molecular weight is 5754.

uU * 0.04167 = nG
uU * 0.007241 = pMol
