<topicdoc>
<name> A - DefinitionHP2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

A = P / P50

Where P is the pO2 (partial pressure of oxygen)
and P50 is the pO2 that produces a saturation
of 50%.

</content>
</topicdoc>

</content>

<name> CO2NORM - DefinitionHP4 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

CO2NORM is a constant and is defined as the arterial
partial pressure of carbon dioxide under normal
physiological conditions.

It has a value of 40mm Hg.

Douglas AR, Jones NL, Reed JW. Calculation of
whole blood CO2 content.
J Appl. Physiol. 65: 473 - 477, 1988

<topicdoc>
<name> HILLCONSTANT - DefinitionHP10 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

HILLCONSTANT has a value of 2.3

HILLCONSTANT is a exponent used in the equation
relating blood oxygen content and blood pO2.
The relationship is described using the sigmoidal
Hill equation:

S = An / 1 + An

where S is the fraction saturation, An is
(PO2SATURATED/P50)^HILLCONSTANT where
PO2SATURATED is the pO2 at 100% saturation
and p50 is the pO2 that produces a saturation
of 1/2 or 50%.

Dill DB, Edwards HT, Florkin M, Campbell RW.
Properties of dog blood.
J Biol. Chem. 95: 143 - 152, 1932.

Hill AV. The possible effects of the aggregation of
the molecules of haemoglobin on its dissociation curves.
J Physiol. 40: iv - vii, 1910.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> PHNORM - DefinitionHP23 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

It is customary to express H+ concentration of
blood on a logarithm scale, using pH units.
pH is related to the actual H+ concentration
by the following formula:

pH = log 1/[H+] = -log[H+]

PHNORM is a constant with a value of 7.40.
This is normal pH of blood.

</content>
</topicdoc>
