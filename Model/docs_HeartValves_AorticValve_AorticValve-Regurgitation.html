<name> AorticValve-Regurgitation - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Valvular lesions can affect the ability of the aortic valve to
function. Valvular lesions can occur as a result of rheumatic
fever and less frequently as a congenital defect. When the edges
of the aortic valve are destroyed so that they cannot close as
the ventricles contract, regurgitation or backflow of blood occurs.
This results in a decrease in the net stroke volume output of
the heart.
