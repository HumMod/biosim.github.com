KAldoEffect.Delayed

Overview

Aldosterone affects the intracellular:extracellular fluid ratio
of potassium. This ratio is determined by the balance of the
sodium - potassium ATPase pump-driven potassium influx, and
potassium fluxes through several different pathways. Aldosterone
causes a shift of potassium into cells.

In HumMod, this effect of aldosterone on potassium flux is
defined by a multiplier named KAldoEffect. At a normal
plasma aldosterone concentration of 300pMol/L, the
KAldoEffect is 1.0. At a concentration of 1500pMol/L,
the KAldoEffect is 1.1.

Young DB, Jackson TE. Effects of aldosterone on potassium
distribution. Am J Physiol R526 - R530, 1982.

Young DB. Quantitative analysis of aldosterone's role in
potassium regulation. Am J Physiol 255: F811 - F822, 1988.
