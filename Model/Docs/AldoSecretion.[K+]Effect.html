AldoSecretion.[K+]Effect

Overview

Aldosterone is an adrenocortical steroid secreted by the
glomerulosa cells of the adrenal cortex. Aldosterone plays
a pivotal role in electrolyte and fluid balance and also
influences acid-base homeostasis. Secretion of aldosterone
is highly regulated and is mainly controlled by the the
extracellular concentration of angiotensin II and potassium,
both of which stimulate its secretion. Aldosterone increases
renal tubular reabsorption of sodium and secretion of potassium.

Potassium Effect

An increase in plasma K+ levels stimulates aldosterone secretion.
In HumMOd, the effect of potassium levels on aldosterone
secretion are expressed as multiples of of base secretion. At a
normal potassium plasma concentration of 4.4mEq/L, the K+Effect on
aldosterone secretion is 1.0. As plasma potassium levels rise,
the K+ effect increases. Data for the curve relating plasma
potassium levels to multiples of aldosterone secretion come from
a variety of papers listed below.

Scholer D, Birkhauser M, Peytremann A, Riondel AM, Vallotton MB,

ACTH and potassium in man. Acta Endocrinol 72: 293 - 307, 1973.
