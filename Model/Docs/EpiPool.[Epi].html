EpiPool.[Epi]

Overview

In 1895, Oliver and Schaefer first showed that a substance
present in the adrenal gland was pressor. This substance was
subsequently shown to be epinephrine.

Oliver G, Schaefer EA. The physiologic effects of extracts
from the suprarenal capsules. J Physiol 18:230-276, 1895

Barger G, Dale HH. Chemical structure and sympathomimetic
action of amines. J Physiol 41:19-59, 1910.

Stimulation of the sympathetic nerves to the adrenal
medullae causes release of both epinephrine and
norepinephrine into the circulation. Catecholamines
exert their effects by stimulating specific protein
receptor sites known as adrenergic receptors. There are
two types of adrenergic receptors, alpha and beta receptors.
Epinephrine excites both types of receptors.

The epinephrine pool or mass is a function of epinephrine
secretion, epinephrine clearance and the volume of distribution
which is the ECFV.

Concentration Units

Epinephrine (C9 H13 N O3) has a molecular weight
of 183. Typical plasma concentration is 40pG/mL.
or 0.22nMol/L (Multiply by 0.00546 to convert pG/mL
to nMol/L).
