PhCells.pH

Definition

It is customary to express hydrogen ion concentration on a
logarithm scale using pH units.

pH = log (1/[H+]) = -log[H+] where [H+] is the hydrogen
ion concentration in Eq/L

[H+] is calculated using pK, SID and pCO2

[H+] = K * (pCO2/SID) or
pH = pK + log10 (SID/pCO2)

Concentration Units

Normal values for pH are as follows:
pH arterial blood 7.4
pH venous blood 7.35
pH cell 7.07

Overview

Intracellular pH regulates many cellular processes,
including cell metabolism, calcium homeostasis, gene
expression, cell motility and contractility as well
enzyme activity. A close regulation of intracellular
pH is therefore necessary to maintain normal cellular
activity.
