Overview

RBCs are produced in the bone marrow. The total
mass of red blood cells is regulated by tissue
oxygenation and the hormone erythropoietin [EPO].
Hypoxia results in an increase in [EPO]production
which enhances RBC production.

RBCs normally circulate an average of 120 days
before being destroyed. As the cells age, the
cell membrane becomes more fragile and the cells
rupture during passage through some tight spot
in the circulation, particularly in the spleen.

Physical Units

Mass mL
Flux mL/min
RBC volume mL

Concentration Units

RBC concentrations have been expressed number per
cubic millimeter.

A typical concentration is 5,200,000 (plus or minus 300,000)
in men and 4,700,000 (plus or minus 300,000) in women.

A typical RBC secretion and clearance rate is 0.014mL/min
