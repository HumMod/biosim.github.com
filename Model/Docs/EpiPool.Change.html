EpiPool.Change

Definition

Changes in the Epi pool or mass are a function of both
gains and losses in epinephrine over time. Gains may
occur from increased secretion due to increased sympathetic
nerve stimulation; from infusion of epinephrine
using the epi pump feature of the model and/or increases
from a pheochromocytoma.

Physical Units

[Plasma] pG/mL or nMol/L
Mass nG or pG

milli = 10^-3
micro = 10^-6
nano = 10^-9
pico = 10^-12

Concentration Units

Epinephrine (C9 H13 N O3) has a molecular weight
of 183. Typical plasma concentration is 40pG/mL.
or 0.22nMol/L (Multiply by 0.00546 to convert pG/mL
to nMol/L).
