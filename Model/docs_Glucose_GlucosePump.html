<topicdoc>
<name> GlucosePump - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

The Glucose Pump enables the model user to change
plasma glucose levels by infusing glucose at different
rates. Data is taken from a variety of human studies
relating infusion rates to plasma concentrations
of glucose.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Glucose - Physical Units </name>
<label> Physical Units </label>
<content>

Plasma Concentration mG/mL, mG/dL or nMol/L
Mass mG
mG/dL * 0.05551 = mMol/L
mG * 5.551 = mMol
mG/mL * 100 = mG/dL
Pump units are mG/Min
Units for glucose movement are mG/Min

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Glucose - Concentration Units </name>
<label> Concentration Units </label>
<content>

Molecular Weight (MW) of glucose is 180

Typical plasma glucose concentration [Glucose] = 1.1mg/mL
The volume of distribution is the ECFV.

Glucose Mass = ECFV * [Glucose]
= 15000 * 1.1 = 16500mG

Typical glucose concentrations under a variety of
physiological situations. Drip is considered the
steady state.

mG/dL mMol/L
----- ------
Hypoglycemia 45 2.5
Starving 60 3.3
Fasting 85 4.7
Drip 110 6.1
Fed 130 7.2
Glucose Load 200 11.1

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Switch - DefinitionGP1 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

When the glucose pump switch is turned on, the model
user can set infusion rates using the sliding bar
feature in the glucose pump display box.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Setting - DefinitionGHP2 </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

When the glucose pump switch is turned on, the model
user can set infusion rates using the sliding bar
feature in the glucose pump display box. Rates of infusion
may be increased from 0 mG/Min, in increments of 10, up to
300 mG/Min; from 300 mG/Min, in increments of 100, up to
1000 mG/min; and from 1000 mG/Min, in increments of 1000
up to 10,000nG/Min.

</content>
</topicdoc>
