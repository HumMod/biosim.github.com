<topicdoc>
<name> [K+] - Overview </name>
<label> Overview </label>
<content>

Extracellular potassium concentration is closely regulated
and is a function of the balance between rates of intake and
excretion of potassium. Maintenance of normal potassium
balance requires the kidneys to adjust their excretion rapidly
and precisely to wide variations in intake.

Control of potassium distribution between the extracellular
and intracellular compartments also plays an important role
in potassium homeostasis. Because over 98 percent of the
total body potassium is contained in the cells, they can serve
as an overflow site for excess extracellular fluid potassium
during hyperkalemia or as a source of potassium during
hypokalemia.


In HumMOd, potassium is divided into extracellular K (KPool)
and cellular K (KCell)

Young DB. Analysis of long term potassium regulation.
Endo Rev 6: 24 - 44, 1985

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Change - KP1Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Changes in the potassium pool or mass are a function of gains
and losses in potassium over time. Gains can occur from potassium
in the diet via reabsorption from the GI lumen; movement of
potassium out of cells ;from intravenous administration or
from transfusion.

Losses in potassium can occur in urine; potassium movement
into cells; from sweating; from hemorrhage and during
dialysis.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Gain - KP2Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Gains in potassium occur from 4 possible sources:

1. Potassium absorption from the GI tract.
2. Movement of potassium out of cells and into the ECF.
3. Potassium in an IV drip.
4. Potassium in a blood transfusion.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> InitialMass - KP3Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The initial mass is the starting amount of potassium. Since the
plasma concentration is 4.4mEq/L (the Target[K+]) and the
volume of distribution is the ECFV (15,000mL), then the
initial mass or circulating pool is 66.

Initial Mass = Target[K+]*ECFV.Vol

</content>
</topicdoc>


<topicdoc>
<name> Loss - KP4Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

Losses in potassium may occur in 5 possible ways:

1. Potassium loss in the collecting duct of the kidney.
2. Movement of potassium out of the ECF into cells.
3. Loss of potassium in sweat.
4. Loss of potassium in a hemorrhage
5. Loss of potassium during dialysis

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Mass - KP5Definition </name>
<label> Defintion </label>
<content>

Mass is the calculated amount of potassium at any given time.
It is the change in potassium integrated over time.

</content>
</topicdoc>

<topicdoc>
<name> Target[K+] - KP6Definition </name>
<label> Definition </label>
<content>

The Target[K+] is the initial plasma concentration of potassium.
Target[K+] = Initial Mass/ECFV.Vol

</content>
</topicdoc>
